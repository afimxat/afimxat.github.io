About Me

I'm a post-doctoral researcher at Diba Lab, focusing on sleep deprivation's impact on memory consolidation. My research involves analyzing extracellular records from the sleep-deprived rat hippocampus during learning.

I hold a BS in Computer Engineering and an MS in Cognitive Science. My Ph.D. work, supervised by Dr. Hulusi Kafaligonul, explored neural mechanisms governing sub-second audiovisual temporal interactions and their role in visual timing and motion perception. Additionally, I contributed to projects on phonological aspects of language acquisition and motion perception during neural aging.
